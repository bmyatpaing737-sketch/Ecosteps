Lampi National Marine Park

The main issue at Lampi National Park is the conflict between conservation and development, driven by threats to biodiversity from human population growth, economic development, and illegal activities like blast fishing.

Challenges include overfishing, habitat loss, and lack of consistent management oversight.

Biodiversity under pressure:

Human activity, development, and illegal practices are threatening diverse ecosystems.

Overfishing and illegal fishing:

Trawlers and blast fishing reduce fish stocks and harm marine life.

Habitat loss:

Land conversion for agriculture contributes to ecological destruction.

Hkakabo Razi National Park

Issues at the park

Commercial hunting, unsustainable use of forest products, and lack of trust in conservation management drive damaging activities such as logging and gathering non-timber forest resources.

These practices contribute to declining wildlife and increased deforestation.

Environmental degradation:

Logging, hunting, and uncontrolled forest use have damaged ecosystems.

Impact on resources:
